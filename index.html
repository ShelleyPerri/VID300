How it works

1
Officer Deploys

An officer places the VID 300 on the vehicle that requires immobilization instead of waiting for a tow truck.

2
Vehicle Secured

The device locks the vehicle in place with a chain‑based system, preventing movement until properly released or towed by the tow truck.

3
Driver Resolves

When the driver returns, they follow agency instructions—typically scanning the QR code, paying fees, or following release steps—without requiring the officer to remain on site.

Agency Benefits

  • Frees Police Resources: Officers immobilize and return to patrol immediately.
  • Reduces Tow Delays: No waiting hours on scene for tow providers.
  • Improves Safety: Keeps roadways and access clear while maintaining control of the vehicle.
  • Compliance: Creates a documented, timestamped process that aligns with new Colorado laws.

Device Highlights

  • Chain‑based immobilization works across car and truck types
  • Unique device IDs for audit and reporting
  • Weather‑resistant lock and QR instruction plate
  • Training materials and deployment guidance included

Law Enforcement Scenarios

DUI or Arrest Situations

When a driver is taken into custody, immobilize the vehicle on scene until a tow truck is available, freeing officers immediately.

Blocked Fire Lanes / Hydrants

Secure illegally parked vehicles quickly to clear emergency access while awaiting removal.

Abandoned or Stolen Recovery

Immobilize vehicles found abandoned or recovered from theft to prevent flight until proper towing or release procedures occur.

Special Events / Traffic Control

Deploy during parades, festivals, or sporting events to enforce restricted zones without tying up patrol staff.
